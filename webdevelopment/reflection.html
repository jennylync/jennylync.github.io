Personal Reflection

Looking Back..

My career began after successfully passing my licensure board exam from the Philippines. I entered the field as a Junior Medical Technologist and learned the basics of the clinical laboratory areas such as Chemistry, Hematology, Clinical Microscopy, Immunology, Serology and Processing. I took small steps toward leading my department involving quality assurance roles and inventory management. During these early years of my career, I built the desire to gain better experiences and opportunities. It led me to make a life-changing decision: to leave my home country. It was never easy to leave my family behind, but I knew that I was doing it not only for my professional goals but also to provide a better future for them.

New Beginnings..

Far away from home, my journey as an Overseas Filipino Worker (OFW) started in Abu Dhabi, a modern city in the middle of the desert. This transition to working abroad wasn’t easy because while working as a generalist, I was also tasked to lead a laboratory department (Microbiology) where I didn’t have enough experience at my first facility. Fortunately, my Pathologist was an expert in the field and was supportive and helped me grow in the role. Through this, I was exposed to regulatory compliance tasks and requirements, accreditation processes, and inspections for clinical laboratories such as the CAP and ISO. Both facilities I worked at in the UAE taught me valuable lessons—how to adapt to diverse teams, to work under pressure, build leadership skills, and improve communication. I believe that these experiences helped me prepare for the job I had always dreamed of and become a better version of myself.

One of my top career goals was working in the U.S. While working in the UAE, I started gathering experience and preparing my documents and eventually got hired by MedPro. In 2021, I arrived in the U.S. and began working as a contingent worker through an agency. I decided to stay at the same hospital (St. Mary’s Hospital) after being offered a full-time position when I finished my agency contract. Since then, I’ve enjoyed a better work-life balance and discovered my area of interest and specialties in the laboratory (Chemistry, Hematology, POCT). I have also continued growing my technical skills with the help of my laboratory leaders and by joining our Clinical Ladder Program, which allows me to take part in projects, community service, and professional development.

Looking Ahead..

Today, I am a graduate student at the University of Missouri, pursuing a Master of Health Science in Clinical Diagnostic Sciences with an emphasis on Clinical Laboratory Science. I’m also taking my leadership elective courses for an Online Educator Graduate Certificate. My overall experience gained me a sense of purpose in my role to deepen my expertise in certain laboratory departments, lead projects, engage in quality improvement tasks, improving continuing education access to laboratory professionals and teaching future laboratory professionals or new laboratory employees. Overall, the journey wasn’t easy, but it was worth it. It has shaped my professional identity and my commitment to high-quality healthcare services.
